üìú History & Legacy

Released in 1998, the Work VS-KF (K-Face) was a performance-oriented evolution of Work's legendary VS-XX design. The "KF" designation referred to its distinctive convex "K-face" spoke profile, which provided additional strength while maintaining minimal weight.

The VS-KF quickly became a favorite in time attack, drift, and street racing scenes throughout Japan and internationally. Its lightweight construction and classic mesh design made it ideal for Nissan S-chassis, Toyota AE86, and early Subaru WRX builds.

Production ended in 2007, though the design's influence continues in Work's current lineup. The VS-KF is considered one of the most iconic JDM wheels of the late 1990s and early 2000s, representing the golden era of Japanese tuning culture.

üí° Collector's Notes

Market Value: Genuine VS-KF wheels in good condition command premium prices due to their racing pedigree and classic status. Look for the Work logo engraved on the face and serial numbers on the inner barrel. Original gold anodized finishes are particularly sought after. Sets typically sell for $2,500-$4,500 USD depending on size and condition. Beware of replicas which lack the quality machining and forged construction.
